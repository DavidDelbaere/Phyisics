What the question looks like:
Small mass m is suspended from an inelastic nonstretchable string
of length L. When Initially at rest, this simple pendulum makes an angle 55° with the veritcal.
Find the force of tension when Ek is at its maximum.

SOLUTION:
To Calculate the force of tension, we need to know where maximum kinetic energy resides:

EP Top = EK Bottom


We know this from the law of conservation of energy.
Now, we can rewrite as:

mgh = 1⁄2mV2


With h representing the change of height from the initial to final position.
Rewriting to solve for velocity at this point, we get

V2 = 2gh


When the mass is at the bottom of the system, our net force (Fnet) is equal to centripial Force (Fc):
Since the mass is rotating with a distance from the centre of length L, we write this as:

Fc = Fnet

Fc = m V2/L

mV2/L = Ft - Fg

mV2/L = Ft - mg

Ft = mV2/L + mg

Ft = m[ V2/L +g ]


Now we subsitiute V2:

Ft = m[ 2gh/L +g ]

Ft = mg[ 2h/L + 1 ]


Now we must find value h, or the change of height:
Using the diagram below, we understand that

Cos(θi) = L-h/L

Figure 1: Comparing positions using triangles


Rearranging, we get:

1 - Cos(θi) = h/L


Putting this back into our original formula, we find that:

Ft = mg[ 2(1 - Cos(θi)) + 1 ]


This will give us the formula:

Ft = mg[3 - 2Cos(θi)]
