What the question looks like:
A block of mass m is pushed a distance x along a frictionless,
horizontal table by a constant applied force of magnitude Fa
directed at an angle θ below the horizontal in the direction of motion.
Determine the work done on the block by each of the forces on the block .
State the work done by the net force on the block.
Enter your answer to the nearest tenth of joule.

SOLUTION:
To Calculate the amount of work done, we must calculate the Net Force
To Do this, let us break our down our components:

X COMPONENTS:

ΣFₓ = FₐCosθ


Y COMPONENTS:

ΣFᵧ = Fₙ - FₐSinθ


In this instance, there is no friction being applied to the object, so the components in the y direction do not matter
Therefore, we can say that:

ΣFₙₑₜ = FₐCosθ


Now, Since the formula for work on forces is

W = ΣFₙₑₜ•x,

We may write this as:

W = x•FₐCosθ
